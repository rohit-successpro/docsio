Resource planning in Successpro connects capacity, skills, and financial impact. When you allocate a resource to a project, you see immediately how that decision affects margins and utilization.

## Understand your capacity

Before you allocate resources, you need to know what capacity you have. Successpro shows you:

- **Total capacity**: How many hours each team member is available to work (based on their work schedule and time off)
- **Allocated capacity**: How many hours are already assigned to projects
- **Available capacity**: How many hours are left to allocate
- **Billable utilization**: What percentage of allocated hours are billable (vs. internal or non-billable work)

This real-time view prevents over-allocation and helps you spot bench capacity that could be assigned to revenue-generating work.

## Allocate resources to projects

1. Go to **Resource Management** and click **Allocations**.
2. Click **New Allocation**.
3. Select the **team member** you want to allocate.
4. Select the **project** they'll work on.
5. Enter the **allocation percentage** or **hours per week** (e.g., 50% or 20 hours/week).
6. Set the **start date** and **end date** for the allocation.
7. Select the **role** or **skill** they'll use on this project (this determines which rate card rate applies).
8. Click **Save**.

The allocation is now active. The team member's available capacity decreases, and project margins update automatically to reflect the new resource cost.

:::tip
Use allocation percentages for long-term assignments and hourly allocations for short-term or variable work. Both update capacity and margins in real time.
:::

## AI-powered allocation suggestions

Successpro can suggest resource allocations based on project needs, team skills, and availability. When you create a new project, the system analyzes:

- Required skills and experience level
- Project timeline and effort estimate
- Current team capacity and utilization
- Historical performance data from similar projects

It then recommends which team members are the best fit. You can accept the suggestion or manually select different resources.

## Track utilization

Utilization shows what percentage of your team's available time is billable (assigned to revenue-generating projects).

1. Go to **Resource Management** and click **Utilization**.
2. View utilization by **team member**, **team**, **skill**, or **project**.
3. Compare actual utilization against your target (e.g., 75% billable).

If someone is under-utilized, you can allocate them to more projects. If someone is over-allocated, you can reduce their allocation or add more team members.

Utilization is calculated in real time, so you always see the current picture.

| Utilization Metric | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Billable utilization | % of time allocated to revenue-generating projects |
| Bench time | % of time not allocated to any project |
| Internal utilization | % of time allocated to internal or non-billable work |
| Over-allocation | Team member assigned to more hours than they have available |

## Bench management

Bench time is capacity that's not allocated to any project. Some bench time is healthy (for training, admin, etc.), but too much bench time is lost revenue.

Successpro tracks bench aging: how long each team member has been on the bench. You can set a target bench time (e.g., 10%) and get alerts when someone exceeds it.

When you see bench time, you can:
- Allocate the person to a new project
- Assign them to internal work or training
- Plan for upcoming projects that need their skills

:::warning
Bench time still costs money (you're paying their salary), but it doesn't generate revenue. Minimize bench time to protect margins.
:::
