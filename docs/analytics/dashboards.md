Dashboards in Successpro give you multi-dimensional visibility into your business. Every metric is connected to your actual project data, so you see real numbers, not estimates.

## Access dashboards

1. Go to **Services Analytics** and click **Dashboards**.
2. View pre-built dashboards or click **New Dashboard** to create a custom one.

Pre-built dashboards include:
- **Revenue Dashboard**: Total revenue, revenue by customer, revenue by project, revenue by billing model
- **Margin Dashboard**: Gross margin, operating margin, margin by project, margin by customer
- **Utilization Dashboard**: Billable utilization, bench time, utilization by team, utilization by skill
- **Project Dashboard**: Project status, project profitability, project health, project timeline
- **Delivery Dashboard**: Delivery status, delivery performance, delivery by team, delivery by region

## Build a custom dashboard

1. Click **New Dashboard** and enter a **name** (e.g., "Executive Dashboard").
2. Click **Add Widget** to add a chart or metric.
3. Choose the **widget type**:
   - **Metric**: A single number (e.g., total revenue, average margin %)
   - **Chart**: A bar chart, line chart, pie chart, or table
   - **Scorecard**: A metric with a target and status indicator

4. Select the **data source** (e.g., Projects, Revenue, Margins, Utilization).
5. Choose the **dimensions** to display (e.g., by project, by customer, by region).
6. Set any **filters** (e.g., only projects in Q4, only billable hours).
7. Click **Save**.

The dashboard now shows your data in real time.

## Key metrics

The most important metrics for services businesses are:

| Metric | What It Means | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Billable utilization | % of team time allocated to revenue-generating projects | 70-80% |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus direct resource costs | 40-60% |
| Operating margin | Gross margin minus overhead | 15-25% |
| Project profitability | Revenue minus all costs for a project | Positive |
| Revenue per resource | Total revenue divided by number of team members | $200k-300k/year |
| Bench time | % of team time not allocated to any project | <10% |

Monitor these metrics weekly to spot trends early.

## Drill-down analysis

Every metric in a dashboard supports drill-down. Click on a number to see the details behind it.

For example:
1. Click on **Total Revenue** to see revenue by project.
2. Click on a **project** to see revenue by customer.
3. Click on a **customer** to see revenue by billing model.

This drill-down capability lets you understand the "why" behind every KPI.

:::tip
Use drill-downs to investigate anomalies. If revenue is down, drill in to see which projects or customers are responsible.
:::

## Filters and comparisons

Every dashboard supports filtering and comparison:

- **Filter by date**: View data for a specific period (this month, last quarter, year-to-date).
- **Filter by dimension**: View data for specific projects, customers, teams, or regions.
- **Compare periods**: Compare this month to last month, this year to last year.
- **Compare dimensions**: Compare revenue by project, by customer, by team.

Use filters to focus on the data that matters most to you.

## Scheduled reports

You can schedule dashboards to be emailed to you or your team on a regular basis.

1. Open a dashboard and click **Schedule Report**.
2. Choose the **frequency** (daily, weekly, monthly).
3. Select the **recipients** (email addresses).
4. Choose the **format** (PDF, Excel, or email with embedded charts).
5. Click **Save**.

The report will be sent automatically on the schedule you set.

## Role-based access

Dashboards respect role-based access controls. Finance teams see financial metrics. Delivery teams see delivery metrics. Leadership sees everything.

When you create a dashboard, you can set who can view it and what data they can see.

:::note
Sensitive financial and customer data is protected by role-based access. Team members only see data relevant to their role.
:::
